Failure Map: Crude Oil Cardiotoxicity

Created by: Martin Forero & Isabella Duque

"Abnormalities in cardiac function were highly consistent, indicating a broadly conserved developmental crude oil cardiotoxicity." — PNAS Study

📈
Altered Variable
Cardiac Homeostasis The electrical signals (ion channels) that control the heart's rhythm.
➔
🐟
Affected System
Large Predator Populations Tunas, Amberjack, Swordfish, and Billfish in the Gulf habitats.
➔
⚠️
Mechanism Failure
Developmental Cardiotoxicity Oil chemicals block heart signals, causing severe Bradycardia and irregular beats.
➔
🌡️
Consequence
Pericardial Edema Fluid builds up around the heart, leading to circulatory collapse and death.
➔
❤️
Solution or Care
Population Recovery Restoring habitats to protect the early life stages of these large predators.

Thinking Routine (Scientific Insights)

1. Broad Impact: Cardiotoxicity is "broadly conserved" across many predatory fish species.
2. Species at Risk: Bluefin Tuna, Yellowfin Tuna, and Greater Amberjack are most vulnerable.
3. Normal State: Regular heart contractions allowing blood to reach developing organs.
4. The Event: Spawning in oiled surface habitats during the Deepwater Horizon spill.
5. Key Finding: Even low-level exposure disrupts the heart's electrical coupling.
6. Failure: The heart cannot maintain its rhythm (Arrhythmia) or its speed (Bradycardia).
7. Direct: Circulatory failure caused by high back-pressure and weak pumping.
8. Cascading Loss: Massive loss of early life stages for Gulf predator populations.
9. Recovery: Long-term monitoring of predator numbers and habitat health.
10. Homeostasis: The oil disables the Automatic control of the heart rate. Because the heart (the effector) is poisoned, the body's Negative Feedback loops fail; they can no longer counteract the damage or return the system to its "normal" state, leading to a complete collapse of balance.
